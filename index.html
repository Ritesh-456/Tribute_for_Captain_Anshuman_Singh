About:

Captain Anshuman Singh was the Regimental Vaidya Officer (RMO) in the 26th Battalion of the Punjab Regiment . He was posthumously awarded the Kirti Chakra, India's second-highest peacetime gallantry award, for his exceptional bravery and determination during a major fire incident in Siachen

Education:

He was born in 1997 in Bardiha Dalpat village in Uttar Pradesh, India. He was educated at the prestigious Chail Military School in Himachal Pradesh and completed his schooling at Rashtriya Military School Chail. He then joined the Armed Forces Medical College (AFMC) in Pune for higher education, where he received rigorous training in military medicine, high altitude medicine and combat injuries. His dedication to learning and excellence set the stage for his future endeavors.
After completing his MBBS, he did a one-year internship in Agra. However, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, his internship was extended. Despite the challenges, he remained committed to his work as a medical professional

Lifestlye:

He served as a Medical Officer in the 26th Battalion of the Punjab Regiment. Due to his deployment, he was sent to Chandan Complex under Operation Meghdoot. Despite harsh conditions and immense challenges, he performed his duties with excellence and showed unwavering dedication to the service of the nation.
Captain Anshuman Singh was married to Smriti Singh, an engineer by profession, on February 10, 2023. The couple had plans for their future, including building a home and having children, which they discussed just a day before his untimely death and Smriti Singh described him as an extremely bright student. His death was a big blow to his family, but they feel consoled by his heroic legacy. His father is a retired Subedar from the Army.

Martyr:

In July 2023, a few months after his martyrdom, Captain Anshuman Singh Sia's wife left for Australia for higher education in structural engineering. Captain Anshuman Singh was serving with 403 Field Hospital of 26 Punjab Battalion on attachment to 26 Madras deployed in Chin Glacier. The area remained inaccessible during the winter months and the troops were exposed to extreme risk while manning the forward posts in inaccessible terrain at an altitude of over 19,000 feet in extreme weather conditions. Apart from manning the designated posts, the troops also carried out regular patrolling to monitor the gaps between the posts. Captain Anshuman Singh, serving as the Regimental Medical Officer, was responsible for providing medical care to all the troops deployed in the area.
At around 3 am on 19 July 2023, a fire broke out in the ammunition depot at Chandan Dropping Zone, Siachen. Captain Anshuman heard the sound of fire and rushed out of his fibre glass hut. Captain Anshuman quickly realised that many of his soldiers were trapped inside and needed to be rescued. Without caring for his own safety, he was fully aware of the risk and immediately rushed forward to rescue his fellow soldiers. Despite the dangerous surroundings and the threat posed by the fire, he fearlessly went into the affected area and was determined to search for survivors and evacuate them to safety. Exhibiting quick thinking and fearless leadership, Captain Anshuman Singh managed to rescue four to five individuals from a nearby fibre glass hut, which was rapidly filling with smoke and was on the verge of catching fire. His calm demeanour and clear instructions helped to evacuate these individuals to safety, reflecting his remarkable ability to remain calm even under pressure. He then noticed that the medical examination room was on fire. He went inside his fibreglass hut to retrieve the medical aid box, however, he was unable to get out as the fire spread and engulfed his shelter due to strong winds. Despite repeated attempts, he could not be rescued. His body was retrieved from the shelter after the fire was extinguished.
